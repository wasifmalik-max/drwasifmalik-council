## Introduction

Neurosurgical precision has reached unprecedented heights with the advent of intraoperative imaging technology. Recent studies demonstrate that **real-time CT imaging during surgery reduces reoperation rates by up to 30%**, fundamentally transforming surgical outcomes. The BodyTom intraoperative CT scanner represents a paradigm shift in surgical precision, enabling surgeons to verify anatomical accuracy while patients remain on the operating table.

In Pakistan's evolving healthcare landscape, where neurosurgical demands continue to rise due to increasing trauma cases and complex pathologies, advanced imaging technology like BodyTom offers revolutionary potential. This portable, battery-powered system brings hospital-grade CT capabilities directly into the operating room, eliminating the traditional delays and risks associated with transferring patients for post-surgical imaging verification.

## What is BodyTom Intraoperative CT?

The BodyTom intraoperative CT scanner, developed by NeuroLogica (a Samsung subsidiary), represents cutting-edge portable imaging technology designed specifically for operating room environments. This **32-slice, full-body CT system** features a comprehensive 2-meter field of view and 60cm bore diameter, accommodating diverse patient populations and complex surgical positioning requirements.

**Key Technical Specifications:**
- **Portable Design:** Battery-powered operation enables seamless OR integration
- **Full-Body Imaging:** 2-meter scanning range covers entire anatomical regions
- **Real-Time Processing:** Immediate image acquisition and reconstruction
- **Sterile Environment Compatible:** Designed for aseptic surgical conditions

The system's mobility distinguishes it from traditional fixed CT scanners, eliminating patient transport risks and maintaining sterile surgical fields. This innovation particularly benefits neurosurgical procedures requiring immediate anatomical verification, including **spinal instrumentation, tumor resection, and complex cranial procedures**.

Unlike conventional post-operative imaging protocols, BodyTom provides instantaneous feedback, enabling surgeons to make real-time adjustments and corrections during the procedure itself, significantly improving surgical outcomes and patient safety.

## Applications in Neurosurgery

BodyTom intraoperative CT addresses critical challenges in modern neurosurgery, particularly in complex procedures where anatomical precision determines success rates. **Spinal instrumentation procedures** represent the primary application, where screw placement accuracy directly impacts patient outcomes and long-term stability.

**Primary Neurosurgical Applications:**
- **Spinal Fusion Surgery:** Real-time verification of pedicle screw placement
- **Tumor Resection:** Immediate assessment of resection margins
- **Trauma Surgery:** Anatomical reduction verification in complex fractures

The technology proves invaluable in **revision surgery prevention**, addressing the significant healthcare burden associated with secondary procedures. Traditional surgical approaches rely on post-operative imaging to identify complications, often requiring additional interventions when corrections could have been made during the initial procedure.

**Complex spinal deformity corrections** particularly benefit from intraoperative imaging, where three-dimensional anatomical relationships require precise instrumentation placement. The immediate feedback loop enables surgeons to optimize hardware positioning before wound closure, dramatically reducing revision rates.

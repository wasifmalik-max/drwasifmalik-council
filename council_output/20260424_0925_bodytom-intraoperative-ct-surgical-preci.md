## Understanding BodyTom Intraoperative CT Technology

The BodyTom system, developed by Neurologica (a Samsung subsidiary), is a revolutionary portable intraoperative CT scanner that brings advanced imaging directly into the operating room. This 32-slice imaging system weighs approximately 1,200 pounds yet maintains remarkable mobility, eliminating the need for dedicated imaging suites. The technology provides a 60-centimeter field of view with scan times under one minute for critical anatomical areas.

Key specifications include adjustable radiation dose settings comparable to traditional CT scanners, ensuring patient safety while delivering high-resolution images. The system's mobility allows seamless integration into existing operating theaters without structural modifications. Real-time imaging capabilities enable surgeons to verify surgical accuracy, confirm complete tumor resection, and assess implant positioning immediately during procedures.

The BodyTom's advanced reconstruction algorithms provide detailed visualization of neural structures, vascular anatomy, and surgical hardware placement, making it invaluable for neurosurgical, spinal, and trauma procedures requiring maximum precision.

## Clinical Applications and Indications

BodyTom intraoperative CT serves multiple critical applications in modern neurosurgery. Primary indications include complex brain tumor resections where real-time verification of complete tumor removal while preserving eloquent brain areas proves essential. Spinal instrumentation procedures benefit significantly from immediate confirmation of pedicle screw placement accuracy.

Trauma neurosurgery represents another crucial application, where rapid assessment of intracranial hemorrhage evacuation and skull fracture repair requires immediate imaging verification. The system proves particularly valuable in stereotactic procedures, ensuring precise targeting of deep brain structures.

Vascular neurosurgery cases, including aneurysm clipping and arteriovenous malformation resections, benefit from immediate post-operative imaging to confirm complete treatment and identify potential complications before patient transport.

## Recognizing When Intraoperative CT Is Essential

Several clinical scenarios demand immediate intraoperative imaging verification. **Critical red flag situations** requiring BodyTom assessment include:

**Incomplete tumor visualization** during resection where traditional landmarks become unclear, necessitating real-time confirmation of resection margins. **Unexpected anatomical variants** discovered during surgery require immediate imaging to adjust surgical approach safely.

**Hardware malposition concerns** during spinal instrumentation procedures demand instant verification to prevent neurological complications. **Intraoperative bleeding** with uncertain source location benefits from immediate CT assessment to guide targeted intervention.

**Loss of neurophysiological monitoring** signals during eloquent area surgery requires urgent imaging to identify potential structural damage. **Unexpected resistance** during tumor removal may indicate proximity to critical structures, warranting immediate CT confirmation.

**Cerebrospinal fluid leakage** during cranial procedures needs prompt assessment to identify dural defects requiring repair. These situations represent surgical emergencies where delayed imaging could compromise patient outcomes significantly.

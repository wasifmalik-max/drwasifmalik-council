## What is BodyTom Intraoperative CT?

The BodyTom intraoperative CT scanner, developed by Neurologica (a Samsung subsidiary), is a state-of-the-art portable imaging system designed specifically for operating room use. This 32-slice CT scanner weighs approximately 1,200 pounds yet maintains remarkable mobility, allowing seamless integration into existing surgical suites without major infrastructure modifications.

Key technical specifications include a 60-centimeter field of view, enabling full-body scanning capabilities within 10 minutes. The system provides high-resolution 3D imaging with exceptional detail, crucial for visualizing delicate neural structures, vascular anatomy, and precise instrument positioning. Its advanced reconstruction algorithms generate images comparable to traditional CT scanners while maintaining the flexibility of intraoperative use.

The BodyTom's unique design features include a self-contained unit with integrated power systems, advanced radiation shielding, and compatibility with surgical navigation systems. This integration allows surgeons to correlate real-time imaging with pre-operative planning, ensuring optimal surgical trajectories and complete lesion removal. The system's mobility enables positioning around patients in various surgical positions, making it invaluable for complex neurosurgical, spinal, and trauma procedures where traditional imaging would be impossible or impractical.

## Applications in Neurosurgical Procedures

BodyTom intraoperative CT finds extensive application across numerous neurosurgical specialties. In brain tumor surgery, real-time imaging enables surgeons to verify complete tumor resection while preserving critical brain functions. The immediate feedback helps identify residual tumor tissue that might be missed with conventional surgical techniques, reducing the need for revision surgeries.

Spinal surgery benefits significantly from intraoperative CT guidance, particularly in complex deformity corrections, instrumentation placement, and tumor resections. The system provides immediate verification of hardware positioning, ensuring optimal screw placement and avoiding potential complications such as neural injury or hardware malposition.

Trauma neurosurgery represents another crucial application, where rapid, accurate imaging can be life-saving. The BodyTom enables immediate assessment of hematoma evacuation completeness, bone fragment positioning, and intracranial pressure changes during emergency procedures. This real-time capability is particularly valuable in polytrauma patients where multiple surgical interventions may be required simultaneously.

Vascular neurosurgery also benefits from intraoperative CT, allowing verification of aneurysm clip positioning, arteriovenous malformation resection completeness, and assessment of potential ischemic complications during complex vascular reconstructions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How does intraoperative CT improve surgical safety?**

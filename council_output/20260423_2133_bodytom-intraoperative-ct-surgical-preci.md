## What is BodyTom Intraoperative CT?

BodyTom intraoperative CT, developed by NeuroLogica (a Samsung subsidiary), is a portable, full-body, 32-slice computed tomography scanner specifically designed for operating room environments. Unlike traditional CT scanners that require patient transport, this innovative system brings advanced imaging directly to the surgical suite.

Key specifications include a 60 cm field of view, full-body scanning capability in under 10 minutes, and high-resolution 3D imaging with sub-millimeter accuracy. Weighing approximately 1,200 pounds, the system remains mobile while providing robust imaging capabilities comparable to fixed CT installations.

The scanner's compact design allows seamless integration into existing operating rooms without requiring structural modifications. Its radiation dose optimization ensures patient safety while maintaining diagnostic quality. The system supports multi-planar reconstructions, 3D volumetric rendering, and real-time image processing, enabling immediate surgical decision-making.

Advanced software integration allows direct communication with navigation systems and surgical microscopes, creating a comprehensive intraoperative imaging ecosystem. The scanner's sterile draping system maintains surgical field integrity while providing unrestricted access to imaging capabilities throughout procedures.

## Causes Requiring Intraoperative CT Imaging

Complex neurosurgical cases necessitate real-time imaging verification due to anatomical complexity and precision requirements. Brain tumor resections require continuous monitoring to maximize tumor removal while preserving eloquent brain areas and critical neural pathways.

Spinal instrumentation procedures, including pedicle screw placement and spinal fusion surgeries, demand precise hardware positioning to avoid neural injury and ensure construct stability. Anatomical variations, particularly in complex spinal deformities, make preoperative planning insufficient for optimal outcomes.

Trauma cases present unique challenges with distorted anatomy, hemorrhage, and unstable patient conditions. Emergency procedures often require immediate imaging assessment to guide surgical decision-making and identify complications.

Revision surgeries involving previous hardware or altered anatomy benefit significantly from real-time imaging guidance. Scar tissue formation and anatomical distortion from previous procedures make intraoperative verification essential for safe and effective outcomes.

## Symptoms and Red Flags Requiring Advanced Imaging

**Immediate surgical red flags** include progressive neurological deterioration, signs of increased intracranial pressure, and acute spinal cord compression. These emergencies demand rapid diagnosis and intervention with real-time imaging support.

**Brain tumor presentations** manifest as progressive headaches, seizures, focal neurological deficits, cognitive changes, and visual disturbances. **Sudden onset of severe symptoms** indicates potential complications requiring immediate imaging verification during surgery.

**Spinal pathology warning signs** include severe back pain with neurological symptoms, bowel/bladder dysfunction, progressive weakness, and **cauda equina syndrome**. These presentations often require complex instrumentation with intraoperative imaging guidance.

**Post-operative complications** present as unexpected neurological changes, persistent pain despite intervention, and signs of hardware failure. **Any deviation from expected post-operative course** warrants immediate imaging assessment to identify potential complications requiring surgical revision.

**Infection signs** including fever, wound drainage, and progressive neurological decline demand urgent evaluation and potential surgical intervention with real-time imaging support.
